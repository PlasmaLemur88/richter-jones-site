A firm built on a narrower premise.

Richter Jones is a Santa Fe business law practice founded in 2014 by David A. Richter and Jeremy R. Jones. We built it deliberately to be small, to accept a limited number of matters, and to practice law in a specific way. This page is about that way.

On why we are small

Most business law firms scale by adding associates and increasing the number of matters they can process. We chose not to. We found that the work we were most proud of — and the work our clients valued most — was the kind that could only be done with careful attention from someone senior. And careful attention from someone senior does not scale.

So we decided not to scale. Today the firm has two partners, one associate, and a handful of long-standing client relationships. We turn away roughly as much work as we accept. This is not a humble-brag. It is an operational constraint that keeps us honest about the quality of the work we do.

On how we think about a matter

When a client brings us a problem, the first thing we try to do is understand what they actually want. This is harder than it sounds. Most clients tell us what they think they want, which is often a specific legal outcome — a particular clause in a contract, a motion filed, a settlement number. What they actually want is usually something broader: their business to be protected, a relationship preserved, a risk reduced, a decision made with confidence.

Once we understand what they actually want, we can often get there without doing the specific thing they asked for. A contract dispute can be resolved by a phone call. A threatened lawsuit can be defused by a single letter. An employment issue can be prevented by a conversation that does not become a conversation with a lawyer.

We are not opposed to the dramatic gestures — motions, depositions, trials, hard-fought negotiations. We do them when they are necessary. We just try not to confuse them with the work.

On the clients we represent

We represent New Mexico businesses — mostly closely-held, some public, none large. Our clients tend to be founders, operators, in-house counsel, and the leaders of family businesses. We have clients we have represented for a decade. We have other clients we have represented for a single matter and then recommended to someone better-suited for their longer-term needs.

We do not advertise. New clients come to us through referral — from other lawyers, from accountants, from our clients, increasingly from the essays we publish. If you have arrived here by one of those paths, welcome. The next step is a conversation.

Signed, David A. Richter & Jeremy R. Jones
