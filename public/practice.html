01

Commercial Litigation

For disputes that cannot be negotiated away.

Most business disputes should be settled, and most of them are. The ones that become litigation are the ones where someone has made a calculated decision that a courtroom is the only way forward. We represent clients in those situations.

Our litigation practice focuses on contract disputes, business torts, partnership dissolutions, and commercial fraud claims in New Mexico state and federal courts. We have tried cases at every level from magistrate court through the Tenth Circuit, and we handle both plaintiff and defense work.

Our approach is built on strategic restraint. We do not file motions to make a record. We do not take depositions to bill hours. We try to understand what the other side actually wants, what they are afraid of, and what the judge is likely to do if we cannot settle. Then we build a strategy around those three things.

02

Business Contracts

The architecture of the deal.

A good contract does not prevent disputes. It determines how they are resolved when they arise. We negotiate, draft, and review commercial agreements for New Mexico businesses, from the one-page vendor agreement to the eight-figure asset purchase.

Our contracts practice covers the full range of business agreements: sales and purchase contracts, master service agreements, vendor agreements, licensing, distribution, joint ventures, and the ancillary documents that accompany closings. We work equally with clients drafting their own agreements and clients reviewing agreements drafted by the other side.

The most important work we do on contracts is the part that happens before anything is signed. We try to understand what the business is actually trying to accomplish, what could go wrong, and what the parties would want if things do go wrong. The document that results from that conversation is almost always simpler and shorter than the one the client expected.

03

Employment Law

Counsel before the complaint.

Employment law is where good intentions become expensive mistakes. Most of the employment disputes we see could have been prevented by a ten-minute conversation that did not happen. We try to be the lawyer clients call before that conversation does not happen.

Our employment practice covers the work that sits on top of the employer-employee relationship: handbooks and policies, wage-and-hour compliance, classification decisions, independent contractor agreements, non-compete and non-solicitation issues, and the increasingly important question of what happens when someone leaves. We represent employers in New Mexico across a range of industries.

When disputes do arise, we handle them. But we would rather be the call that helps you structure something correctly the first time than the call that tries to unwind it after a demand letter has arrived.

Business Formation

The company is the document.

The operating agreement is the constitution of the company that will grow up around it. We spend a lot of time making sure founders understand this before they sign anything.

Our formation practice covers entity selection (LLC versus S-corp versus C-corp versus something more exotic), operating agreement drafting, founder equity agreements, early-stage financing documents, and the corporate housekeeping that founders tend to postpone until it matters. We work with New Mexico businesses from the first conversation through the point at which they need bigger-firm representation.

The most common request we get from founders is to review a document someone else drafted. The most common thing we tell them is what the document is actually saying, which is almost always different from what they thought it was saying.
