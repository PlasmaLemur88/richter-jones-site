Contracts & Business Agreements

Agreements that hold because they were built to hold.

Jeremy R. Jones drafts and negotiates commercial agreements for New Mexico businesses at every stage — from a first operating agreement to a multi-party acquisition. The work is not filling in templates. It is understanding what the parties actually need, where the risks live, and drafting language that will still make sense when things do not go according to plan.

Most contract disputes are not about interpretation. They are about things that were not written down clearly enough when the relationship was good. The indemnification clause seemed unimportant. The definition of "material breach" felt obvious. The remedy provision was borrowed from another deal. And then something went wrong, and the parties discovered that their agreement was not the agreement they thought they had made.

Jeremy R. Jones practices contract law with that failure mode in mind. The drafting process is a discipline of identifying risk allocation before anyone has a reason to care about it. Who bears the loss if the supplier fails to deliver? What triggers the right to terminate, and what happens to the deposits when it does? What does "reasonable efforts" mean in a provision that actually matters? These are not pedantic questions. They are the questions that determine the outcome of disputes that have not happened yet.

Operating agreements receive particular attention. They govern what happens when partners disagree, when an owner wants out, when someone dies, and when the business faces a liquidity event. A well-drafted operating agreement does not just describe the structure — it anticipates the friction points and provides mechanisms to navigate them without litigation. A poorly drafted one turns a business dispute into a personal one, and a personal one into an expensive lawsuit.

We handle the full range of commercial agreements: vendor contracts, services agreements, NDAs, licensing arrangements, real estate purchase contracts, and the more complex documents that accompany mergers, acquisitions, and financing rounds. We review incoming agreements too — the ones presented by the other side, where the drafting was done by someone whose client is not you. Before you sign something that will govern a material relationship, you should know what you are signing.
